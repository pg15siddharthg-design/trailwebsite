Frequently Asked Questions

When should I bring my newborn for the first checkup?
Ideally within 3–5 days after birth, especially after discharge from hospital, to ensure feeding, weight gain and overall health are on track.
What is the vaccination schedule for babies?
Vaccinations start at birth and continue through childhood as per the Indian Academy of Pediatrics (IAP) schedule. Your pediatrician will guide you with a personalized plan.
My child has frequent cough and cold. Is this normal?
Yes, children can have 6–8 episodes per year, especially if they attend school. However, persistent or severe symptoms should be evaluated.
My child is not eating properly. What should I do?
Poor appetite is common in children. Focus on a balanced diet, avoid force-feeding, and consult a pediatrician if growth is affected.
When should I worry about fever in my child?
Fever above 100.4°F in infants under 3 months, or persistent high fever in older children, should be evaluated by a doctor.
How do I know if my child’s growth is normal?
Growth is monitored using weight, height and head circumference charts. Regular pediatric visits help track this accurately.
Do you provide newborn and infant care?
Yes, comprehensive care for newborns and infants including feeding guidance, growth monitoring and vaccination is provided.
When should developmental milestones be checked?
Milestones are checked during routine visits. Early identification of delays helps in timely intervention.
